Phonology

Vaellish — Sound System & Alphabet Correspondence

Alphabet Correspondence — English → Vaellish
Vowels
Five Short Vowels — Always Clipped, Never Drawn Out
Vaellish uses five vowels, all pronounced short and sharp. No long vowels, no diphthongs, no silent vowels.
A — "ah" as in "cat" E — "eh" as in "bed" I — "ih" as in "bit" O — "oh" as in "hot" U — "uh" as in "but"
Consonant Clusters
KH — Guttural Fricative
Produced at the back of the throat. Similar to "ch" in Scottish "loch."
khavel — eight
VR — Voiced Labio-velar
Clipped "vr" blend — V immediately into rolled R.
vroth — six
DR — Voiced Stop Cluster
Hard D immediately followed by rolled R, no vowel between.
drak — self / I
STR / STN — Triple Clusters
Up to three consonants at word start, each pronounced distinctly.
sternv — truth striv — flow
Stress & Rhythm
First-Syllable Stress — Always
Stress falls on the first syllable of every word without exception.
VEL-na FOR-mir-ak
No Silent Letters / No Double Vowels
Every letter is pronounced. Adjacent vowels are separated by a glottal stop.

Quick Grammar Reference
Sentence Structure — SOV
Subject → Object → Verb. Verb always last.
drak krat-en velk-na
I [other] see = I see them
Verb Tense Suffixes
-ka Past   -na Present   -sha Future   -oth Command
Noun Case Suffixes
(none) Subject   -en Object   -to Possession   -vel Location
Plurals & Adjectives
-ir Animate plural   -en Inanimate plural
